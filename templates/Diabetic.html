Diabetic Wound Care

Tips for Diabetic Wound Care

    ]
  • Keep pressure off the wound. Walk using crutches if the wound is on your foot
  • The wound will not hurt. That does not mean it is better. Use your eyes to evaluate if it is getting better or worse
  • Keep the wound clean and dry
  • Wash hands before and after dressing changes
  • Use mild soap and warm water to clean the wound
  • Avoid using alcohol, hydrogen peroxide, or iodine on the wound
  • Apply antibiotic ointment or cream
  • Cover the wound with a clean bandage or dressing
  • Change the dressing daily
  • Monitor the wound for signs of infection, such as redness, swelling, or pus

Seek Medical Attention If...

  • Increased pain, redness, or swelling around the wound.
  • Fever or chills.
  • Pus or drainage from the wound.
  • Foul odor coming from the wound.
  • Worsening of the wound or failure to heal.
